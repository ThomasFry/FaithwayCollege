About us

The school, which is situated on the edge of Himeville village at the foothills of the Southern Drakensberg Mountains, offers tuition from Grade 1 to Grade 12

Faithway College was founded in 1994 by a group of families who wanted an education based on Christian principles for their children. Accordingly, they founded the school attached to the Highlands Church. Soon afterwards it separated from the Highlands Church, and was able to purchase a portion of the farm Riversdale from the insolvent estate of Allen Robertson, just large enough to erect school buildings and have a number of playing fields. It now has a Foundation Phase building, an Intermediate Phase School building and a High School building

From these small beginnings Faithway has developed into a school which has been able to achieve outstanding results in the educational field. We offer tuition from grade 1 – 12. Faithway follows the syllabus and curriculum requirements of the KwaZulu-Natal Education Department with whom it is registered and we write the National Senior Certificate Matric exam . All the major core subjects are offered as well as a range of specialised subjects such as Agricultural science and Consumer Studies, both of which are in demand in our district . Instruction is given in English, and English is taken at a Home Language level. Afrikaans and isiZulu are offered at a First Additional Language level. We are members of ISASA and fully accredited with UMALUSI

We consistently achieve 100% pass rate in the Matric exams and are often awarded the trophy for the top achieving school in the district . A high proportion of our pupils have gained a university entrance pass In addition to our excellent matric pass rate, with many Bachelor Passes, learners have participated in Olympiads, Art and Essay Competitions with many achieving outstanding results. Learners have received scholarships to attend various prestigious schools and academies, such as, the Simon’s Town Naval Academy and Northwood. One learner received a scholarship for Masters in Anthropology at Oxford, whilst in 2018 we had the top matriculant in the entire district who went on to study psychology on a full bursary. We also had the privilege of teaching the youngest graduate of Spar Management Academy. This is just the tip of the iceberg. We have university students and graduates excelling in the fields of Politics, Law, Journalism, Actuarial Sciences, Medicine and Education

The small numbers in each age group, together with our rural location, have made it difficult to compete with schools in academic, cultural and sporting activities. Despite this, our learners and educators have risen to the challenge, and have participated and excelled in a wide range of activities such as Choir, Debate, chess, rugby, soccer, cricket, netball and hockey. We also offer Archery as a sport. In the fields of sport and culture our learners excel, receiving District and Sisonke colours in athletics. A number have been invited to participate in Sisonke hockey. We have had 2 cricketers and 1 rugby player participating at the SALGA games. On the cultural scene Faithway has received a number of awards over the years for both Choir and Debating. We have a learner winning the Ghandi essay competition, and others being published by the Poetry Institute of South Africa. Students have been selected to attend Rotary Leadership camps, become exchange students to Germany and to address the government as part of the Children’s Parliament

As the school grows more parents in the district are coming to realise that it is no longer necessary to send their children away to boarding school because there is a fully functioning high school with a sound academic record on their doorstep. It is a place where each boy or girl is encouraged to strive to reach his or her full potential, not only in the academic sphere but also in a wide range of cultural and sporting activities. We also try to inculcate civic awareness so that everyone learns to care for others and to show compassion for those in need. A large number of our own pupils fall into that last category themselves having experienced our harsh life can be after the loss of both parents.

Faithway is a friendly, happy school where there is structure, discipline and order with a good rapport between staff and pupils
